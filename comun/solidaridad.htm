Irish Republican Socialist Committees of North America Oppose the French Show Trials!

On behalf of the Irish Republican Socialist Movement, the Irish Republican Socialist Committees of North America have issued the following statement condemning the upcoming show trials by the French government against leftist political activists. In France, a dozen Spanish activists are being held under preventive detention orders, keeping them imprisoned without trial. Along with other exceptional measures, their preventive detention may be extended up to four years without trial. Seven of them have been held since July 2002 and will finally go to trial on 16 November 2005, nearly three and a half years since their arrests. Two others, currently at liberty, will also go on trial at the same time.

These trials are in accordance with the Fortress Europe agenda, where European states, in this case France and Spain, are cooperating to eradicate all threats to the status quo resulting from working class resistance. The accused in these cases have been targeted because of their political activism against European capitalism and imperialism, and the French government cannot hide the fact that it wants to convict them solely on that basis. Their crime is being communists and anti-imperialists. These trials are reminiscent of the show trials Irish republican and republican socialist activists have experienced in Ireland, Britain, and elsewhere.

From the Sallins train robbery case where members of the IRSM were arrested, tortured, and convicted without evidence; to the supergrass trials using paid perjurers; to the arrest and imprisonment of three IRSM members in France in 1982; to the trials of the Birmingham Six, Guildford Four, etc., where merely being Irish was accepted as proof of guilt; these trials are nothing new to us and we extend our solidarity to the men going on trial in Paris. We call on all radical and progressive political activists to oppose these trials and support the activists who are going on trial. It's vitally important that we all do whatever we can to support them.

The show trials are set to begin on 16 November 2005 at 9am and will be held in Paris' notorious Palais de Justice. Nine sessions will take place, all of which will be held on Wednesday mornings, and Thursday and Friday evenings, until 2 December. Solidarity protests and pickets are already being planned across the globe. Protest letters, faxes, and emails are also being organised.
